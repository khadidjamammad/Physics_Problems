## 8. Lorentz Force

### Step 1: Use the correct formula

For a charged particle moving in a magnetic field:

$$
F = qvB\sin\theta
$$

Since the particle moves perpendicular to the magnetic field:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

and

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

So:

$$
F = qvB
$$

---

### Step 2: Substitute the values

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)
$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate

$$
2 \times 0.5 = 1
$$

$$
10^{-19} \times 10^6 = 10^{-13}
$$

So:

$$
F = 1 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{N}
$$

---

### The Answer

$$
\boxed{F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{N}}
$$
