## 1. Gauss's Law

**Problem Statement:** A point charge of $+2 \text{ C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1 \text{ m}$ centered at the origin.

### Given Data:
* **Charge ($q$):** $+2 \text{ C}$
* **Radius ($r$):** $1 \text{ m}$
* **Permittivity of free space ($\varepsilon_0$):** $\approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

### Theory:
According to **Gauss's Law**, the net electric flux ($\Phi_E$) through any closed surface is proportional to the enclosed electric charge:

$$\Phi_E = \frac{q_{encl}}{\varepsilon_0}$$

> **Note:** The flux is independent of the radius of the sphere; it only depends on the total charge enclosed within the surface.

### Calculation:
Substitute the values into the formula:

$$\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}$$

$$\Phi_E \approx 2.258 \times 10^{11} \text{ V}\cdot\text{m}$$

### Final Answer:
The total electric flux is **$2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ V}\cdot\text{m}$**.
