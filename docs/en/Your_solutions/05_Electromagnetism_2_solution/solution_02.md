## 2. Ampere's Law

**Problem Statement:** Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \text{ A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

### Given Data:
* **Current ($I_1$ and $I_2$):** $5 \text{ A}$
* **Distance between wires ($d$):** $10 \text{ cm} = 0.1 \text{ m}$
* **Distance to midpoint ($r$):** $5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}$
* **Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$):** $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

### Theory & Direction:
Using the **Right-Hand Rule**, we determine the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) from each wire at the midpoint:
1. **Wire 1 (Current Up):** Field points **into** the page at the midpoint.
2. **Wire 2 (Current Down):** Field also points **into** the page at the midpoint.

Since both fields point in the same direction, the total magnetic field is the sum of the two:
$$B_{total} = B_1 + B_2$$

### Calculation:
The formula for the magnetic field of a long wire is:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

Since $I$ and $r$ are the same for both wires:
$$B_{total} = 2 \times \left( \frac{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{2\pi \times 0.05} \right)$$

1. Cancel $2\pi$:
$$B_{total} = 2 \times \left( \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{0.05} \right)$$
2. Simplify:
$$B_{total} = 2 \times (2 \times 10^{-5})$$
$$B_{total} = 4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$$

### Final Answer:
The magnetic field at the midpoint is **$4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$**, directed **into the page** (assuming the first wire's current is upward).
