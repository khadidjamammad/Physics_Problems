## 4. Magnetic Torque

**Problem Statement:** A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10 \text{ cm}$ by $5 \text{ cm}$ carries a current of $2 \text{ A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.3 \text{ T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. What is the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop?

### Given Data:
* **Dimensions:** $10 \text{ cm} \times 5 \text{ cm} \rightarrow (0.1 \text{ m} \times 0.05 \text{ m})$
* **Current ($I$):** $2 \text{ A}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.3 \text{ T}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (between the magnetic field and the **normal vector** of the loop)

### Theory:
The torque ($\tau$) on a current-carrying loop in a uniform magnetic field is calculated using:
$$\tau = N \cdot I \cdot A \cdot B \cdot \sin\theta$$

* **$N$**: Number of turns (assumed to be 1 here).
* **$A$**: Area of the loop ($length \times width$).
* **$\theta$**: The angle between the magnetic field and the area normal. Since $B$ is parallel to the plane, it is $90^\circ$ from the normal.

### Calculation:
1. **Calculate Area ($A$):**
   $$A = 0.1 \text{ m} \times 0.05 \text{ m} = 0.005 \text{ m}^2$$

2. **Calculate Torque ($\tau$):**
   $$\tau = 1 \times 2 \times 0.005 \times 0.3 \times \sin(90^\circ)$$
   $$\tau = 2 \times 0.005 \times 0.3 \times 1$$
   $$\tau = 0.01 \times 0.3$$

### Final Answer:
The magnitude of the magnetic torque is **$0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$**.
