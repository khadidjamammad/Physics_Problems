## 5. Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

**Problem Statement:** We have a parallel-plate capacitor with the following parameters:
* **Area ($S$):** $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
* **Distance ($d$):** $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
* **Voltage ($V$):** $500 \text{ V}$

---

### 1. Calculate the capacitance $C$
**Formula:** $C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 S}{d}$
* $C = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02}{0.005}$
* **Result:** $C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}$ (or **$35.4 \text{ pF}$**)

### 2. Calculate the energy $W$ stored
**Formula:** $W = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$
* $W = 0.5 \times (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) \times (500)^2$
* $W = 0.5 \times (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) \times 250,000$
* **Result:** $W \approx 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}$ (or **$4.43 \text{ μJ}$**)

### 3. Calculate the electric field intensity $E$
**Formula:** $E = \frac{V}{d}$
* $E = \frac{500}{0.005}$
* **Result:** $E = 100,000 \text{ V/m}$ (or **$10^5 \text{ V/m}$**)

### 4. Calculate the force of attraction $F$ between plates
**Formula:** $F = \frac{1}{2} Q E$  (where $Q = CV$)
* First, find $Q$: $Q = (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) \times 500 = 1.77 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}$
* $F = 0.5 \times (1.77 \times 10^{-8}) \times 100,000$
* **Result:** $F \approx 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$
