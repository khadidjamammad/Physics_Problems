## 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)

**Problem Statement:** A light ray travels from air ($n = 1.00$) into glass ($n = 1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

### Given Data:
* **Index of refraction (Air), $n_1$:** $1.00$
* **Index of refraction (Glass), $n_2$:** $1.50$
* **Angle of incidence, $\theta_1$:** $30^\circ$

### Theory:
**Snell's Law** describes the relationship between the angles of incidence and refraction when light passes through the boundary between two different media:
$$n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$$

### Calculation:
1. Substitute the known values:
   $$(1.00) \sin(30^\circ) = (1.50) \sin\theta_2$$
2. Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
   $$0.5 = 1.50 \sin\theta_2$$
3. Isolate $\sin\theta_2$:
   $$\sin\theta_2 = \frac{0.5}{1.5} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333$$
4. Solve for $\theta_2$ using the inverse sine ($\arcsin$):
   $$\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.333)$$

### Final Answer:
The angle of refraction is approximately **$19.47^\circ$**. 
*(Note: The light bends toward the normal because it is entering a more dense medium.)*
